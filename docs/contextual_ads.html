Contextual Advertising System

Multimodal ad targeting from content understanding—semantic retrieval and timestamp-level relevance without relying on user-level tracking.

Problem

User-level tracking faces privacy and regulatory pressure; brand safety still needs relevance. Content-aware placement requires understanding video, audio, and text jointly and scoring ads at the right moment—not just the right page.

Approach

Encode segments of the media stream into a shared embedding space, index ads the same way, and match with similarity plus metadata filters. Rank candidates and align insertion to high-relevance timestamps.

System design

Video Content
   ↓
Frame + Audio Extraction
   ↓
Multimodal Encoding (Video + Audio + Text)
   ↓
Segment-Level Embeddings
   ↓
Vector DB (Milvus / FAISS)
   ↓
Metadata Store (PostgreSQL)
   ↓
Ad Embedding Index
   ↓
Similarity Matching
   ↓
Timestamp-Level Ad Placement

Key components

1. Multimodal understanding

  • Parallel video, audio, and text features
  • Joint embedding space for retrieval

2. Embedding and indexing

  • Segment-level vectors for fine-grained targeting
  • Milvus or FAISS for ANN search

3. Hybrid retrieval

  • Vector similarity for semantics
  • Postgres for filters (brand rules, geography, etc.)

4. Matching and placement

  • Ranked ad lists per segment
  • Timestamp-aligned delivery

Techniques

  • Multimodal learning
  • ANN search (FAISS / Milvus)
  • Semantic matching and re-ranking

Outcomes

  • Privacy-preserving targeting (content, not cookies)
  • Fine-grained relevance at segment level
  • Operational path to scale with standard vector DB patterns
