Multi-Language Lip-Sync (VisualDub)

Generative pipeline from audio to lip motion: mel features, 3D keypoints, and warping-based rendering with identity preservation across languages.

Problem

Dubbing across languages changes phoneme timing and mouth shape; naive viseme mapping looks uncanny. Production systems need temporal coherence, stable identity, and realistic motion—not just a single-frame swap.

Approach

Encode audio with a strong temporal model, predict 3D facial motion and dense fields, then warp or decode frames with generators that preserve identity. Train with audio-visual pairs so the network learns language-agnostic motion cues.

System design

Input Audio
   ↓
Audio Feature Extraction (Mel Spectrogram)
   ↓
Audio Encoder
   ↓
3D Keypoint Prediction
   ↓
Motion Estimation Network
   ↓
Warping / Rendering Network
   ↓
Final Video Frames

Key components

1. Audio processing

  • Mel-spectrogram front end
  • Temporal encoding for alignment

2. 3D keypoint modeling

  • Facial keypoints and head pose
  • Expression control for natural speech

3. Motion estimation

  • Dense motion fields
  • Keypoint-driven deformation

4. Warping and rendering

  • Feature warping with motion
  • SPADE-style or GAN decoding for photorealism

5. Identity preservation

  • Consistent identity across frames
  • Stable under expression change

Techniques

  • Generative models (GAN family)
  • 3D keypoint and motion transfer
  • Audio-visual learning
  • SPADE / warping networks

Outcomes

  • Cross-lingual lip sync with improved realism
  • Temporal consistency for long-form clips
  • Deployed in production (VisualDub)

Related open source

Face generation and animation work in METALIA, ImprovedMotion-with-ref, and Generalizable-Gaussian-Avatar complements this direction.
